One of most influential battles in the history of the Roman Empire is the Battle of Carrhae in 216 BCE. This battle saw the hastati, principes, and triaii face off against the Carthaginian soldiers, likely in phalanx formation. The Silver Shields may have even been present.

Another important battle in history is the Battle of Hastings in 1066 AD. In this battle, the Normans faced the Anglo-Saxons. The Normans utilized the mounted knight, which was unlike any foe that the Anglo-Saxon shield walls had faced. The Anglo-Saxons had just defeated a Norwegian army at the Battle of Stamford Bridge that year. I conjecture that the Anglo-Saxon army was battered and as they faced the Normans at Hastings, it would have been quite difficult for them to actually win in their tired state.
